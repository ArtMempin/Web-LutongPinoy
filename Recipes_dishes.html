Chicken Adobo

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (about 1 kg) chicken (legs, thighs, or a mix)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (white or cane vinegar)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic, minced (about 5-6 cloves)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns (or freshly cracked black pepper)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Marinate the Chicken:

  • In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns.
  • Add the chicken and mix well to coat. Let it marinate for at least 15-30 minutes (or up to a few hours for more flavor).

2. Sear the Chicken:

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
  • Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade for later) and sear the chicken pieces until browned on all sides (about 5 minutes).

3. Cook the Adobo:

  • Pour the reserved marinade and water into the pot with the chicken.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  • Cover and simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally.

4. Adjust Seasoning:

  • Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

5. Serve:

  • Transfer the chicken adobo to a serving dish and spoon the sauce over the top.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice.

Menudo

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) pork shoulder or belly, diced into small cubes
  • 1/2 lb (225g) pork liver, diced into small cubes
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 small bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional, for added umami)
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for sautéing

Instructions:

1. Marinate the pork and liver:

  • In a bowl, combine the diced pork and liver with soy sauce and a bit of salt and pepper.
  • Set aside for about 15-30 minutes to marinate.

2. Sauté the aromatics:

  • Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add the garlic and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add the onions and cook until softened.

3. Cook the tomatoes:

  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and become saucy, about 3-5 minutes.

4. Add the tomato sauce and water:

  • Pour in the tomato sauce and water (or broth).
  • stir to combine.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the pork is tender.

5. Add vegetables:

  • Add the potatoes, carrots, bell pepper, and raisins (if using).
  • Continue to simmer until the vegetables are cooked through.

    • 6. Adjust seasoning:

    • Taste and add more salt and pepper as needed.

    7. Serve:

    • Serve hot with rice.

    Dinuguan

    Ingredients:

    • 1 lb (450g) pork (shoulder, belly, or butt), diced
    • 1/2 lb (225g) pork blood (available at Asian markets)
    • 1 cup water or broth
    • 2-3 tbsp vinegar
    • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • 2-3 green chili peppers (e.g., siling pangsigang or jalapeño)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Cooking oil for sautéing

    Instructions:

    1. Sauté the aromatics:

    • Heat oil in a pot over medium heat.
    • Add garlic and onion, sauté until softened and fragrant.

    2. Cook the pork:

    • Add diced pork to the pot and sauté until browned on all sides.
    • Add water or broth and bring to a boil.
    • Reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork is tender.

    3. Add the blood and vinegar:

    • Slowly add the pork blood, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
    • Stir in vinegar and let it simmer for a few more minutes.

    4. Adjust seasoning:

    • Add salt and pepper to taste.
    • Add green chili peppers for spice, if desired.

    5. Serve:

    • Serve hot with steamed rice or puto (Filipino rice cake).
