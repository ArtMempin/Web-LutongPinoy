Leche Flan

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1/4 teaspoon lemon or orange zest for added flavor

Equipments Needed:

  • Llanera
  • Steamer or Large Pot with Lid
  • Mixing Bowls
  • Whisk or Electric Mixer

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Caramel:

  • In a saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water.
  • Place over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves.
  • Once dissolved, stop stirring and let it boil until it turns a golden amber color. Watch closely to prevent burning.
  • Quickly pour the caramel into the llanera or your chosen mold(s), tilting to evenly coat the bottom. Set aside to let the caramel harden.

2. Make the Flan Mixture:

  • In a large mixing bowl, gently whisk the egg yolks until well combined. Avoid creating too many bubbles.
  • Add the sweetened condensed milk and evaporated milk to the eggs, mixing until smooth.
  • Stir in the vanilla extract and optional zest, ensuring everything is well incorporated.
  • Important: To prevent lumps, strain the mixture through a fine sieve before pouring it into the caramelized mold.

3. Steam the Flan:

  • Cover the llanera or molds with aluminum foil to prevent water droplets from falling onto the surface of the flan.
  • Arrange the molds in a steamer or a large pot with a steaming rack. Ensure that the water below does not touch the flan.
  • Steam over medium heat for about 30-45 minutes. To check for doneness, insert a toothpick or knife into the center; it should come out clean.
  • Once done, remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.

4. Chill and Serve:

  • After cooling, refrigerate the leche flan for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow it to set fully.
  • To unmold, run a knife around the edges, place a serving plate over the mold, and invert it quickly. The caramel will drizzle over the flan, creating a glossy finish.
  • Slice and serve chilled. Enjoy it as is or with a dollop of whipped cream or fresh fruits.

Puto Bumbong

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (preferably pirurutong, a purple glutinous rice, or mix in food coloring if unavailable)
  • 1/2 cup white glutinous rice (optional, for a softer texture)
  • Purple food coloring (if using white glutinous rice)
  • 1/4 cup water or as needed
  • Grated coconut (fresh)
  • Muscovado sugar or brown sugar
  • Butter or margarine

Equipments Needed:

  • Steamer
  • Bamboo Tubes
  • Cheesecloth or Muslin Cloth

Instructions:

1. Soak the Rice:

  • Rinse and soak the purple glutinous rice and white glutinous rice (if using) in water for 3–4 hours, or preferably overnight. If you cannot find purple glutinous rice, use regular glutinous rice and add a few drops of purple food coloring later on.
  • After soaking, drain the rice and grind it using a food processor or blender until it reaches a slightly coarse, moist consistency. If the mixture is too dry, add a little water, a tablespoon at a time.

2. Form the Mixture:

  • Transfer the ground rice mixture into a bowl. If you used white glutinous rice, mix in the purple food coloring until the desired shade is reached.
  • Prepare the bamboo tubes by wrapping them with cheesecloth, leaving enough hanging over the edge to cover the top of the mixture later. Lightly grease the inside of each bamboo tube with butter or margarine.
  • Fill each bamboo tube with the rice mixture, about three-quarters full to allow for expansion during cooking.

3. Steam the Puto Bumbong:

  • Fill your steamer with water and bring it to a boil. Place the bamboo tubes in the steamer, standing upright. Cover with a lid.
  • Steam for about 20–25 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and firm to the touch.

4. Serve:

  • Once cooked, remove the bamboo tubes from the steamer and allow them to cool slightly before gently removing the puto bumbong from the tubes.
  • Top with freshly grated coconut, a sprinkle of muscovado or brown sugar, and a pat of butter or margarine.
  • Serve warm and enjoy!

Halo-Halo

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shaved ice (or crushed ice if unavailable)
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • Sweetened saba bananas (plantains cooked with brown sugar)
  • Sweetened jackfruit (langka)
  • Sweetened red beans
  • Sweetened white beans (optional)
  • Macapuno (sweetened coconut strips)
  • Nata de coco (coconut jelly)
  • Kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • Leche flan (Filipino-style caramel flan)
  • Ube halaya (purple yam jam)
  • Jelly cubes (gulaman or grass jelly)
  • Sweetened corn (optional)
  • Pinipig (toasted young rice or crispy rice flakes)
  • Ice cream (typically ube, but you can also use vanilla or other flavors)
  • Langka (jackfruit strips)
  • Sugar or condensed milk (optional:to add more sweetness)
  • Cheese (grated, optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Ingredients:

  • Sweetened fruits and beans: You can buy pre-sweetened fruits and beans in jars, or make them at home by simmering the fruits (like saba bananas and jackfruit) with sugar and water until tender and syrupy.
  • Leche flan: If you want to include leche flan in your halo-halo, you can either make it from scratch or buy it pre-made. Check out my Leche Flan recipe if needed.
  • Ube Halaya (Purple Yam Jam): Make or buy ube halaya. It adds a beautiful pop of color and flavor.

2. Assemble the Halo-Halo:

  • In a tall glass or a large bowl, layer the sweetened fruits and beans at the bottom. You can use a mix of any of your favorite ingredients—saba bananas, sweetened jackfruit, red beans, macapuno, nata de coco, etc.
  • Add a layer of shaved ice on top, about halfway to three-quarters full.

3. Add the Evaporated Milk:

  • Pour evaporated milk over the shaved ice, allowing it to soak through the ice. You can adjust the amount depending on how creamy you want your halo-halo to be.

4. Add the Toppings:

  • Top the shaved ice and milk with a spoonful of ube halaya, a small slice of leche flan, and a scoop of ube ice cream (or your preferred flavor).
  • Sprinkle some pinipig (crispy rice flakes) on top for added texture.
  • You can also add grated cheese or drizzle some condensed milk for an extra sweet and creamy twist.

5. Mix and Enjoy:

  • Serve the halo-halo with a spoon and mix it well before eating, ensuring all the ingredients and ice are blended together.

Bananacue

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe saba bananas (firm, not overripe)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cups cooking oil (for deep frying)
  • Skewers (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the bananas:

  • Peel the saba bananas and set them aside.
  • You can slice them in half or keep them whole depending on your preference.

2. Heat the oil:

  • In a deep pan or wok, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.

3. Fry the bananas:

  • Once the oil is hot, carefully add the saba bananas.
  • Fry them for about 3-5 minutes until they start to turn golden brown.

4. Caramelize the sugar:

  • Lower the heat and gradually add the brown sugar into the pan.
  • Stir gently to coat the bananas evenly.
  • The sugar will melt and stick to the bananas, creating a caramel-like coating.

5. Continue frying:

  • Fry for another 2-3 minutes until the bananas are fully coated in the melted sugar and have a nice caramelized glaze.

6. Remove and drain:

  • Using tongs or a slotted spoon, remove the caramelized bananas from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

7. Skewer (optional):

  • While still hot, you can skewer the bananas with bamboo sticks for easier handling, just like how it's typically served by street vendors.

8. Serve:

  • Let the bananacue cool slightly before serving.

Fruit Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) fruit cocktail, drained
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple chunks (optional)
  • 1 cup nata de coco (coconut gel), drained
  • 1 cup kaong (sugar palm fruit), drained
  • 1 cup fresh or canned mango chunks (optional)
  • 1 can (7.6 oz) Nestlé cream or heavy cream, chilled
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup shredded young coconut (optional)
  • 1 cup cheese, diced (optional but traditional for added flavor contrast)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the fruits:

  • Drain the canned fruit cocktail, nata de coco, and kaong thoroughly.
  • You can also add fresh pineapple chunks, mango, or other fruits of your choice and then set all the fruits aside.

2. Mix the cream and milk:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the chilled cream and sweetened condensed milk.
  • Stir them together until they are fully mixed and smooth.
  • You can adjust the sweetness by adding more or less condensed milk.

3. Add the fruits:

  • Gently fold the drained fruit cocktail, nata de coco, kaong, fresh fruits (if using), and shredded young coconut into the cream mixture.
  • Mix until the fruits are evenly coated with the cream.

4. Add cheese (optional):

  • If you like a sweet-salty contrast, add small cubes of cheddar cheese to the mix. This is a unique Filipino touch!

5. Chill:

  • Cover the fruit salad and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours (or overnight) to let the flavors meld and for the salad to firm up.

6. Serve:

  • Once chilled, give it one last gentle stir, and serve cold.

Champorado

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit rice)
  • 5 cups water
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder (or 4-5 pieces tablea, Filipino chocolate tablets)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Evaporated milk or condensed milk (for drizzling on top)
  • Optional: Tuyo (salted dried fish), for serving on the side

Instructions:

1. Cook the rice:

  • In a pot, combine the glutinous rice and water.
  • Bring it to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom.
  • Once it boils, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer.
  • Cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice becomes soft and sticky.

2. Prepare the chocolate:

  • While the rice is cooking, dissolve the cocoa powder (or melt the tablea) in a little warm water to form a smooth paste. This ensures no lumps in the porridge. Set aside.

3. Add the chocolate to the rice:

  • Once the rice is soft and cooked, add the cocoa mixture into the pot. Stir well to combine and cook for another 5-10 minutes until the chocolate is fully incorporated and the champorado thickens.

4. Sweeten:

  • Add sugar and a pinch of salt to balance the flavors. Stir and taste, adjusting the sweetness as needed.

5. Serve:

  • Ladle the champorado into bowls and drizzle with evaporated milk or condensed milk on top for added creaminess and sweetness.

6. Optional pairing:

  • Some enjoy champorado with tuyo (salted dried fish) on the side, as the salty-sweet combination is a popular pairing in Filipino cuisine.
